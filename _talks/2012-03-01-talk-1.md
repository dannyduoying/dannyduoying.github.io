Abstract: Space-filling designs play a fundamental role in computer experiments, yet their construction remains challenging. Existing approaches typically rely on optimizing space-filling criteria over a large design space, which can be computationally intensive in practice. To mitigate this issue, some methods restrict the design space by imposing combinatorial structures, such as orthogonal arrays. However, comparable constructions are not available for designs with more refined stratification properties, including general strong orthogonal arrays. In this paper, we introduce a double stratification enumerator that characterizes weighted pairwise similarities among design points and establish its key theoretical properties. We show that the double stratification enumerator of a design is a linear combination of its stratification pattern, which leads to more efficient computation of the latter. We further show that, when averaging over some special level permutations, a broad class of space-filling criteria, including the centered $L_2$-discrepancy criterion and the maximum projection criterion, admit a linear representation in terms of the double stratification enumerator. Motivated by these results, we propose a two-step construction procedure for generating space-filling designs with enhanced stratification properties. Numerical studies demonstrate that the proposed method consistently outperforms existing approaches based on state-of-the-art optimization algorithms and orthogonal array constructions.
